MrBeast: YouTube’s Resident Philanthropist
Early Beginnings

Gaming Videos

Most, if not all of MrBeast’s oldest videos that haven’t been deleted are gaming videos. His very first visible video is a video of the game Minecraft where he defeats mobs to survive a Saw trap[1], shown on the right. Below are several of the games MrBeast played and the content featured in videos of them:

  • Minecraft - In MrBeast’s Minecraft videos, he covered add-ons to the Minecraft experience, like mods[2], player skins[3], and different maps and builds[4].
  • Pokémon Showdown - A fanmade battle simulator spun off from the original Pokémon games[5]; MrBeast usually posts battles here with underused (or UU) Pokémon[6], though sometimes, he plays in random battles[7] (or RB) too.
  • Black Ops 2 - One of the shooting games MrBeast played; he usually posted videos of his matches on the game with his commentary[8].

How Much Money Does X Make?

After about a year of making gaming videos, MrBeast made a series of videos discussing how much money different YouTubers make on YouTube, with the first being titled: "How Much Money Does PewDiePie Make?????"[9], shown on the right.

PewDiePie was, at the time, the most subscribed-to content creator on YouTube, which justifies MrBeast’s interest with his potential income from the platform. PewDiePie would play a bigger part later on in MrBeast’s history.

Along with PewDiePie, he also covered YouTubers like Kyr Speedy and his crew[10], CaptainSparklez[11], Tobuscus[12], Skydoesminecraft[13], Element Animation[14], penguinz0[15], and even himself[16].

MrBeast6000

MrBeast’s YouTube username used to be MrBeast6000, sometimes undergoing several name changes, but remained MrBeast6000 to keep it easily searchable[17]. He even had an outro song sung by Whobilly[18], shown on the right.

After making strictly the two types of videos shown above on his channel, he started making more and more random videos. One of these videos involved Twitch streamers reacting to generous donations, all from the same person[19], which served as unintentional foreshadowing for the content MrBeast does now. Other videos of his around the time are short films, one of which ended up being the first appearance of Chris Tyson[20], a regular to MrBeast’s modern videos.

After a bit more experimentation when it comes to videos, he strikes gold.
